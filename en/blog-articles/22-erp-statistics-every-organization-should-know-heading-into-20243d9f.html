22 ERP Statistics Every Organization Should Know Heading into 2024

Written by ERP Content Team | 11/28/2023
There is no shortage of statistics on the enterprise resource planning (ERP) software market, Not surprising, given that Statista has projected the ERP market to reach a whopping $49.38 billion in 2023.

“It is undeniable that ERP has become an indispensable tool for companies,” says Moovago “ERP statistics are important because they provide valuable insights into the implementation and usage of ERP systems in organizations. By analyzing these statistics, businesses can better understand the benefits and challenges of implementing an ERP solution, as well as identify trends and best practices in ERP adoption.”

Here are 22 ERP Statistics we found in 2023 that every organization should know going forward into 2024:

        1.           1. ERP revenue is expected to show an annual growth rate (CAGR 2023-2028) of 4.78 percent, resulting in a market volume of $62.36 billion by 2028. (Source: Statista)

  2.           2. The average spend per employee in the ERP Software market is projected to reach $14.19 in 2023. (Source: Statista)
  3.  
  4.           3. The U.S. market will account for more than half of the global ERP software market with $25.3 billion in 2023. (Source: Statista)

  6.           4. 45 Percent of ERP projects go over budget. (Source: SelectHub)

  8.           5. The average return on investment (ROI) for an ERP project is 52 percent – which means that for every $1 invested in an ERP system, there is an average return of $1.52. (Source: Parsimony)
  9.  
  10.           6. The total cost of ownership (TCO) for an ERP system is $15,000 to $20,000 per user. Generally, the TCO includes hardware, software, licensing, implementation, and support costs. (Source: Parsimony)

  12.           7. The typical payback period for an ERP investment is 2 to 3 years. The amount of time may vary depending on your organization’s size and complexity, but on average, most businesses report a payback period of around two or three years. (Source: Parsimony)

  14.           8. 66 percent of organizations report that their ERP systems have helped increase efficiency. With an ERP system, companies can streamline processes, automate data entry and reporting, and utilize resources better. (Source: Parsimony)

  20.           11. In that same survey, nearly all (96 percent) of respondents indicated that their ERP system was either “very successful” or “somewhat successful” in reducing costs in purchasing and inventory control areas. (Source: Techaisle)
